## Quality Standards Philosophy

Quality is non-negotiable. Quality standards define what excellent work looks like. Every person is responsible for quality. Excellence requires systematic quality assurance and continuous improvement.

**Core Principle**: Quality is built in, not inspected in. Prevention is better than detection. Everyone owns quality.

## Defining Quality

**Quality means**:
- Meets or exceeds customer expectations
- Defects are rare or eliminated
- Consistency across deliverables
- Fitness for purpose
- Reliability and dependability
- Excellence is visible

**Dimensions**:
- Functional quality (does it work?)
- Design quality (is it well-designed?)
- Reliability (does it work consistently?)
- Usability (is it easy to use?)
- Customer satisfaction (are they happy?)

## Quality Standards

### Establishing Standards

**Quality standards define**:
- What acceptable quality looks like
- Specific criteria
- Measurable outcomes
- Process requirements
- Acceptance criteria
- Review procedures

**Process**:
1. Define standards
2. Communicate clearly
3. Train people
4. Measure compliance
5. Adjust as needed

### Standard Types

**Quality standards cover**:
- Product/service specifications
- Process requirements
- Documentation standards
- Customer communication
- Problem resolution
- Continuous improvement

### Standard Documentation

**Each standard includes**:
- What standard is
- Why it exists
- How it's measured
- Acceptance criteria
- Review process
- Responsibility

## Quality Assurance

### Approach

**Quality assurance**:
- Planned and systematic
- Prevents defects
- Catches issues early
- Continuous monitoring
- Continuous improvement

### Quality Gates

**At each stage**:
- Clear quality criteria
- Review/inspection process
- Approval required
- Defects caught early
- Learning captured

**Benefits**:
- Defects caught early (cheaper)
- Prevention focus
- Quality consistency

### Inspections and Testing

**Quality checks**:
- Regular inspections
- Thorough testing
- Review processes
- Sampling
- Continuous monitoring

### Metrics

**Quality measured by**:
- Defect rate
- Customer satisfaction
- On-time delivery
- Rework rate
- Compliance rate
- Process adherence

## Root Cause Analysis

**When quality issues occur**:

1. **Identify**: What went wrong?
2. **Analyze**: Why did it happen?
3. **Root Cause**: What's the fundamental cause?
4. **Solution**: How do we prevent it?
5. **Implement**: Put solution in place
6. **Verify**: Does it work?
7. **Document**: Capture learning

## Continuous Improvement

**Quality improves through**:
- Regular review of metrics
- Identification of improvement opportunities
- Testing of improvements
- Implementation of winners
- Documentation of learning
- Sharing of best practices

## Quality Culture

**Building quality culture**:
- Leadership commitment
- Clear standards
- Training and support
- Recognition for quality
- Safe reporting
- Continuous improvement focus
- Pride in work

**Culture says**: Quality is everyone's responsibility

## Prevention vs. Detection

### Prevention Focus

**Better to**:
- Design quality in
- Prevent defects
- Train for quality
- Use proven processes
- Inspect/test before delivery

**Cost**: Lower rework

### Detection Focus

**When**:
- Find defects after delivery
- Repair or rework
- Lose customer trust
- More expensive
- Damage reputation

**Cost**: Much higher

## Supplier Quality

**Quality extends to suppliers**:
- Vendor selection criteria
- Quality agreements
- Performance monitoring
- Regular audits
- Continuous improvement
- Relationship management

**Principle**: Quality throughout supply chain
