## Operational Health Definition

Operational health is the overall condition of operational systems and processes. Healthy operations run smoothly, deliver quality, and continuously improve. Organizational health depends on operational health.

**Core Principle**: Monitor operational health proactively. Intervene before problems become crises.

## Health Indicators

### Process Health

**Process is healthy when**:
- On-time delivery
- Quality standards met
- Efficiency targets hit
- Low defect rate
- Smooth execution
- Minimal bottlenecks
- Quick issue resolution

### System Health

**Systems are healthy when**:
- Reliable and stable
- Performing as expected
- No critical issues
- Adequate capacity
- Good uptime
- Data accurate
- Well-maintained

### Team Health

**Team is healthy when**:
- Engaged and motivated
- Skills adequate
- Resources available
- Communication clear
- Support provided
- Development happening
- Stress manageable

### Customer Health

**Customers are healthy when**:
- Satisfied with deliverables
- Receiving value
- Issues resolved
- Communication good
- Retention strong
- Referrals happening
- Relationship growing

## Health Monitoring

### Daily Monitoring

**Daily checks**:
- Key metrics
- System uptime
- Critical issues
- Process flow
- Team status
- Quality alerts

**Purpose**: Early problem detection

### Weekly Reviews

**Weekly assessment**:
- Weekly metrics trend
- Performance vs. target
- Issues and resolutions
- Risk assessment
- Team update
- Customer feedback

**Purpose**: Trend identification

### Monthly Assessment

**Monthly review**:
- Monthly performance
- Trend analysis
- Strategic assessment
- Staffing adequacy
- System review
- Continuous improvement assessment

**Purpose**: Systemic understanding

### Quarterly Deep Dive

**Quarterly analysis**:
- Quarterly performance
- Strategic progress
- Market changes
- Competitive position
- Risk assessment
- Major adjustments needed?

**Purpose**: Strategic adjustment

## Red Flags

**Watch for**:
- Missed deadlines (chronic)
- Quality degradation
- Escalating costs
- Team turnover
- Customer complaints
- System failures
- Mounting backlog
- Resource shortage

**Response**: Investigate and intervene

## Corrective Action

### Issue Identification

**When problem identified**:
1. Confirm the issue
2. Assess severity
3. Understand scope
4. Identify root cause
5. Determine impact

### Response

**Action depends on severity**:
- **Minor**: Normal improvement process
- **Significant**: Task force
- **Crisis**: Emergency response

## Preventive Maintenance

### System Maintenance

**Scheduled maintenance**:
- Equipment servicing
- Software updates
- System optimization
- Database maintenance
- Security patches
- Infrastructure review

**Purpose**: Prevent failures

### Process Refinement

**Regular review**:
- Process performance
- Bottleneck identification
- Efficiency opportunities
- Risk reduction
- Continuous improvement

**Purpose**: Sustained health

## Operational Risk

### Risk Categories

**Risks include**:
- System failure
- Quality issues
- Cost overruns
- Resource shortage
- Skill gaps
- Supply chain disruption
- External changes
- Compliance risks

### Risk Management

**For each risk**:
- Probability assessment
- Impact assessment
- Mitigation strategy
- Contingency plan
- Monitoring
- Regular review

## Scaling Operations

**As volume increases**:
- Processes may need refinement
- Systems may need upgrade
- Team may need expansion
- Complexity increases
- More coordination needed

**Monitor**: Ensure health as scale changes

## Operational Standards

### Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

**Define**:
- Service levels
- Uptime requirements
- Response times
- Quality standards
- Support hours
- Escalation procedures

**Monitor**: Track performance vs. SLA

## Operational Review Process

### Standard Review

**Regular reviews**:
1. **Metrics Review**: Performance vs. targets
2. **Issue Review**: Problems and resolutions
3. **Process Review**: Effectiveness of processes
4. **System Review**: Technology and systems
5. **Team Review**: Capability and engagement
6. **Risk Review**: Risk assessment
7. **Improvement Review**: Improvements implemented
8. **Planning**: Next period planning

**Frequency**: Monthly or quarterly
