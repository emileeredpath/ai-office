## Recognition Philosophy

Recognition celebrates contribution and reinforces values. It's powerful motivator and tool for culture building. Regular, sincere recognition builds engagement and retention.

**Core Principle**: Recognition should be frequent, specific, sincere, and connected to values and objectives. It's not about money but about genuine acknowledgment of contribution.

## Recognition Principles

### 1. Specific

Recognition names:
- Specific behavior or achievement
- Why it matters
- Impact created
- Values demonstrated

**NOT**: "Great job!"
**YES**: "Your detailed analysis on the Q3 proposal identified critical risks, which prevented us from making a costly mistake. That's the kind of thoroughness and accountability we value."

### 2. Timely

Recognition is given:
- Close to achievement
- While impact fresh
- Quickly when noticed
- Regular cadence

**Avoid**: Waiting for annual review to recognize contribution

### 3. Sincere

Recognition is:
- Genuine
- Not perfunctory
- Felt
- Authentic
- Heartfelt

**Avoid**: Generic recognition; recognition that seems forced or insincere

### 4. Appropriate Level

Recognition matches:
- Achievement size
- Impact level
- Contribution importance
- Role expectations

**Calibration**:
- Everyday excellence: Regular acknowledgment
- Notable achievement: Special recognition
- Exceptional impact: Significant celebration

### 5. Aligned with Values

Recognition highlights:
- How values were demonstrated
- Cultural examples
- Desired behaviors
- Strategic alignment

**Connection**: Recognition teaches culture

### 6. Frequency

Recognition is:
- Regular
- Ongoing
- Not rare
- Woven into culture
- Normal practice

**Recommended**: Multiple recognitions weekly organization-wide

## Recognition Programs

### Formal Recognition Program

**Elements**:
- Clear criteria
- Nomination process
- Selection process
- Recognition moment
- Reward (if applicable)
- Communication

**Process**:
1. **Announce**: Criteria and process
2. **Nominate**: Colleagues suggest recipients
3. **Select**: Panel reviews and selects
4. **Celebrate**: Public recognition event
5. **Reward**: Award, bonus, or special opportunity
6. **Share**: Story shared organization-wide

### Peer Recognition System

**Platform or process for peers to recognize each other**:
- Online tool or board
- Regular sharing
- Public visibility
- Easy to use
- Encourages frequent recognition

**Benefits**:
- Accessible to all
- Frequent recognition
- Peer-driven
- Culture building
- Builds relationships

### Spot Awards

**Immediate recognition**:
- For exceptional contribution
- Can be manager or leadership
- Immediate award (small bonus, time off, special project)
- Recognition event
- Story shared

**Benefits**:
- Timely
- Meaningful
- Memorable
- Motivating
- Unexpected

### Annual Recognition Events

**Organization-wide celebration**:
- Awards ceremony
- Achievement celebration
- Milestone recognition
- Team acknowledgment
- Special honors

**Elements**:
- Formal event
- Leadership participation
- Multiple categories
- Stories shared
- Celebration energy
- Memorable experience

## Recognition Diversity

**Recognize different contributions**:
- Performance excellence
- Innovation and creativity
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Development of others
- Overcoming challenges
- Values demonstration
- Long-term commitment
- New employee contribution
- Quiet contributions often overlooked

**Avoid**: Only recognizing visible or high-profile contributions

## Recognition Pitfalls

**Avoid**:
- Insincere or generic recognition
- Delayed recognition
- Recognizing only high performers
- Same people always recognized
- Recognizing only major accomplishments
- Recognition without specific reference
- Forgetting to recognize effort
- Making recognition political
- Only formal recognition
- Recognition that seems like obligation

## Creating Recognition Culture

**Organization builds recognition culture by**:
- Leadership modeling
- Manager training
- Creating recognition opportunities
- Recognition tools and platforms
- Stories of recognition
- Celebrating celebration
- Making recognition normal
- Values connection
- Frequency

**Recognition culture is intentional, not accidental.**

## Recognition and Performance

**Recognition**:
- Motivates continued performance
- Reinforces desired behaviors
- Clarifies expectations
- Builds engagement
- Improves retention
- Creates positive culture

**Lack of recognition**:
- Reduces motivation
- Damages engagement
- Signals misalignment
- Increases turnover
- Weakens culture
