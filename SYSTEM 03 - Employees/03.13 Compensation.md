## Compensation Philosophy

Compensation reflects commitment to employees and values their contribution. Fair, transparent, competitive compensation attracts and retains talent. Compensation aligned with performance motivates excellence.

**Core Principle**: Compensation should be market-competitive, internally fair, performance-based, and transparent. It should attract and retain talent while supporting organizational financial health.

## Compensation Principles

### 1. Market Competitive

**Compensation is**:
- Competitive with market
- Attracts talent
- Prevents poaching
- Fair to candidates
- Sustainable

**How**:
- Regular market analysis
- Benchmark role appropriately
- Consider industry standards
- Consider geography
- Adjust periodically

### 2. Internal Equity

**Compensation is**:
- Fair across organization
- Transparent (or clear guidelines)
- Consistent application
- No unjustified gaps
- Defendable

**How**:
- Clear levels and bands
- Consistent methodology
- Regular review
- Transparent rationale
- Fair application

### 3. Performance-Based

**Compensation reflects**:
- Individual performance
- Contribution
- Results achieved
- Value created
- Potential

**How**:
- Base reflects level and market
- Performance bonuses
- Equity incentives (if applicable)
- Raises based on performance
- Clear connection

### 4. Transparent

**Employees understand**:
- How compensation is set
- What determines raise/bonus
- How they compare (internally)
- Market position
- Path to higher compensation

**How**:
- Clear salary bands (optional)
- Transparent process
- Clear criteria
- Communication
- Accessibility of information

## Compensation Components

### Base Salary

**Fixed compensation**:
- Core income
- Predictable
- Reflects role and level
- Based on market
- Based on experience
- Market adjusted annually

**Determination**:
- Market analysis
- Role requirements
- Experience level
- Performance history
- Internal equity
- Financial capacity

### Performance Bonus

**Variable compensation for performance**:
- Annual bonus (typical)
- Based on individual and organizational performance
- Clear criteria
- Communicated in advance
- Tied to goals and metrics
- Discretionary or formulaic

**Structure**:
- Individual objectives (40-60%)
- Organizational performance (40-60%)
- Clear calculation
- Payout timing (typically annual)

**Amounts**: Typically 10-25% of base salary (varies by level)

### Equity

**Long-term incentive**:
- Stock options or equity grants
- Vesting over time
- Alignment with long-term success
- Retention mechanism
- Not all roles (applicable for key roles)

**Purpose**:
- Long-term alignment
- Retention
- Wealth creation
- Commitment

### Benefits

**Comprehensive benefits package**:
- Health insurance
- Retirement savings
- Life insurance
- Disability insurance
- Paid time off
- Professional development
- Other benefits

**See**: 03.14 Benefits and Wellbeing

## Salary Levels and Bands

### Role Leveling

**Each role has level**:
- Entry/Junior
- Mid
- Senior
- Lead/Management

**Factors**:
- Responsibility
- Experience needed
- Market value
- Reporting level
- Impact level

### Salary Bands

**Each level has range**:
- Minimum: Entry point
- Mid-point: Market average
- Maximum: Experienced in role

**Positioning**:
- Entry level: Near minimum
- Developing: Mid-low
- Proven: Mid
- Expert: Mid-high to maximum
- Exception: Exceptional circumstances

**Raises**: Movement within band based on performance and tenure

## Annual Compensation Review

### Timing

**Annual review process**:
- Typically January-March
- Aligned with fiscal year
- Aligned with performance reviews
- Budget planning
- Market adjustment

### Process

1. **Market Analysis**: Update market data
2. **Performance Review**: Review individual performance
3. **Budget Planning**: Determine available budget
4. **Recommendation**: Manager recommends increase
5. **Approval**: Leadership reviews and approves
6. **Communication**: Communicating to employee
7. **Implementation**: Raise effective date

### Raise Determination

**Considers**:
- Market movement
- Individual performance
- Tenure in role
- Career progression
- Equity and consistency
- Budget constraints
- Internal relationships

**Typical Ranges**:
- Cost-of-living adjustment: 2-3%
- Solid performance: 3-5%
- Strong performance: 5-8%
- Exceptional performance: 8%+
- Promotion: Larger increase (10%+ typically)

## Promotion Compensation

### Promotion Increase

**When promoted**:
- Base salary increases
- Reflects new level
- Accounts for previous salary
- Considers market for new level
- Typically 8-20% increase

**Timing**:
- Typically effective on promotion date
- Reflects increased responsibility
- May be staged if significant jump

## Performance Bonus

### Bonus Determination

**Individual Performance Bonus**:
- Based on individual goals achievement
- Based on performance rating
- Clear criteria communicated annually
- Typical payout: December or Q1

**Organizational Performance Bonus**:
- Based on organizational/company results
- Typically company revenue, profit, or goals
- Shared across appropriate employees
- Reflects organizational success

**Calculation**:
- Individual target bonus % (e.g., 15% of salary)
- Performance rating multiplier (e.g., 50-150%)
- Organizational performance multiplier
- Final bonus amount

### Bonus Communication

**Communicated**:
- At beginning of year
- Target bonus amount or percentage
- Goals and metrics
- Calculation methodology
- Payout timing

## Compensation Strategy by Level

### Individual Contributor

**Compensation**:
- Base salary: Market-based
- Performance bonus: Yes (typically 10-15%)
- Equity: If applicable to role
- Benefits: Full

**Growth**: Through performance in role or promotion

### Team Lead

**Compensation**:
- Base salary: Higher level
- Performance bonus: Yes (typically 15-25%)
- Equity: If applicable
- Benefits: Full

**Growth**: Through people and performance

### Manager

**Compensation**:
- Base salary: Significant step up
- Performance bonus: Yes (typically 20-30%)
- Equity: If applicable
- Benefits: Enhanced

**Growth**: Through organizational impact

### Executive

**Compensation**:
- Base salary: Market-based
- Performance bonus: Yes (typically 30-50%+)
- Equity: Significant
- Benefits: Comprehensive

**Growth**: Through strategic execution

## Compensation and Retention

**Fair compensation**:
- Reduces turnover
- Improves engagement
- Attracts talent
- Reduces poaching
- Supports retention

**Underpaying**:
- Increases turnover
- Creates resentment
- Reduces engagement
- Attracts less talent
- Damages morale

## Pay Equity

### Commitment

Organization is committed to:
- Pay equity
- No discrimination
- Fair and equal compensation
- Regular review
- Correcting inequities

### Process

**Regular review**:
- Analyze compensation by role and level
- Check for gender, race, other gaps
- Investigate differences
- Correct where unjustified
- Document

## Confidentiality and Transparency

### Balance

**Organization balances**:
- Transparency (understanding how compensation works)
- Privacy (specific salaries may not be disclosed)

**Transparency Includes**:
- Clear levels and ranges
- Basis for determination
- Process
- How to advance compensation

**Privacy Includes**:
- Specific individual salaries private (as person wishes)
- Market data confidential

## Equity and Fairness

**All compensation decisions**:
- Fair and defensible
- Consistent with policy
- Free from discrimination
- Document rationale
- Reviewable

## Compensation Strategy

### Overall Strategy

**Compensation strategy**:
- Attracts and retains talent
- Supports performance
- Maintains equity
- Supports financial sustainability
- Competitive with market
- Performance-focused

**Reviewed**: Annually or as needed
