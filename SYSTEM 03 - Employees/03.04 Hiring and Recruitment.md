## Hiring Philosophy

Hiring is strategic investment in organizational capability. Each hire shapes organizational culture, capability, and trajectory. We hire for fit with organization, not just skills.

**Core Principle**: We hire for values alignment and capability; we believe we can train skills but not values. Each hire is decision with long-term implications.

## Hiring Process

### Phase 1: Planning

**Before Opening Position**:

1. **Need Justification**
   - Why do we need this position?
   - How does it serve strategy?
   - What results do we expect?
   - What's the business case?

2. **Role Definition**
   - What is the role?
   - What are responsibilities?
   - What success looks like
   - What qualifications needed

3. **Compensation Planning**
   - What is market rate?
   - What can we afford?
   - What are we offering?

4. **Timeline Planning**
   - When do we need to fill this?
   - What is realistic timeline?
   - Who is involved in hiring?

**Output**: Position specification; hiring plan; approval

### Phase 2: Recruitment

**Attracting Candidates**:

1. **Job Description**
   - Clear role description
   - Responsibilities
   - Success criteria
   - Qualifications
   - Compensation and benefits
   - How to apply

2. **Sourcing**
   - Post internally first
   - Use networks
   - Use job boards
   - Recruit directly
   - Use recruiters if needed

3. **Screening**
   - Initial resume review
   - Phone screening (30 min)
   - Alignment check
   - Interview-ready candidates

**Screening Criteria**:
- Required qualifications met
- Values alignment signals
- Capability indicators
- Communication quality
- Enthusiasm for role

**Output**: Shortlist of candidates to interview

### Phase 3: Interviewing

**Interview Process** (varies by level):

**Round 1: Initial Interview**
- Structure: 30-60 minutes
- Interviewer: Team lead or direct manager
- Topics: Background, experience, motivation, values fit
- Outcomes: Pass/no pass decision

**Round 2: Detailed Interview**
- Structure: 45-90 minutes
- Interviewer: Manager and potential team member
- Topics: Role-specific skills, problem-solving, team fit
- Outcomes: Capability assessment

**Round 3: Leadership Interview** (for senior roles)
- Structure: 45-90 minutes
- Interviewer: Executive leadership
- Topics: Strategic thinking, leadership, vision alignment
- Outcomes: Senior leadership assessment

**Round 4: Reference Checks** (before offer)
- Purpose: Validate capability and culture fit
- References: Previous managers and colleagues
- Questions: Performance, reliability, teamwork, values

### Phase 4: Selection

**Decision Process**:

1. **Debrief**: Interviewers share assessments
2. **Discussion**: Evaluate fit and capability
3. **Decision**: Recommend or reject
4. **Approval**: Final approval from hiring authority
5. **Offer**: Extend offer to selected candidate

**Decision Criteria**:
- Capability for role
- Values alignment
- Team fit
- Growth potential
- Overall assessment

### Phase 5: Offer and Onboarding

**Offer Letter**:
- Position and title
- Reporting relationship
- Compensation
- Start date
- Benefits
- Contingencies

**Onboarding Preparation**:
- Workspace setup
- System access
- First-day materials
- Welcome plan
- First week agenda

**Output**: Signed offer; new hire ready for day one

## Hiring Criteria

### Capability Requirements

**For each role**:
- Required skills and experience
- Technical capabilities
- Domain knowledge
- Experience level
- Specific qualifications

### Values Alignment

**We assess for**:
- Excellence orientation
- Integrity and honesty
- Learning and growth
- Collaboration approach
- Clarity in communication
- Accountability mindset
- Respect for others
- Innovation openness

**How We Assess**:
- Behavioral interview questions
- Problem-solving scenarios
- Reference feedback
- Cultural fit assessment

### Growth Potential

**We consider**:
- Capability for growth
- Learning orientation
- Career trajectory
- Development potential
- Leadership potential (if appropriate)

### Team Fit

**We assess**:
- Compatibility with team
- Collaborative approach
- Communication style
- Work style fit
- Cultural add

## Interview Questions

### Capability Assessment

**Questions**:
- "Tell me about a complex problem you solved."
- "Describe a project where you had significant impact."
- "What is your experience with [specific skill]?"
- "How do you approach learning new things?"
- "Tell me about your technical depth."

### Values and Culture Fit

**Questions**:
- "Tell me about a time you had to compromise on something important."
- "Describe a situation where you had to act with integrity."
- "How do you handle feedback?"
- "Tell me about a time you collaborated effectively."
- "Describe your approach to continuous learning."

### Motivation and Fit

**Questions**:
- "What attracted you to this role?"
- "What are you looking for in your next opportunity?"
- "How does this role fit your career goals?"
- "What excites you about our organization?"
- "What questions do you have about the role?"

## Diversity and Inclusion

### Commitment

We commit to:
- Diverse candidate pools
- Equitable evaluation
- Bias awareness
- Inclusive hiring
- Equal opportunity

### Practices

**To ensure diversity**:
- Recruit from diverse sources
- Use structured interviews
- Diverse interview panels
- Clear evaluation criteria
- Unconscious bias training

### Goal

Build team that:
- Reflects diverse perspectives
- Brings different experiences
- Values different viewpoints
- Creates inclusive environment
- Drives innovation through diversity

## Hiring Standards

### Quality Over Speed

- Don't rush hiring
- Better to wait for right person
- Cost of bad hire exceeds recruitment
- Cultural fit matters
- Long-term success matters

### Consistency

- Same standards applied to all
- Clear decision criteria
- Documented process
- Fair treatment
- Equitable compensation

### Rigor

- Thorough assessment
- Multiple perspectives
- Careful evaluation
- Clear decision rationale
- Appropriate escalation

## Contingent Offers

Offers may be contingent on:
- Background check
- Reference verification
- Skills assessment
- Credential verification
- Health requirements (if applicable)

**Process**:
1. Conditional offer extended
2. Contingencies completed
3. Final offer
4. Start date confirmed

## Hiring Metrics

**Track**:
- Time to hire
- Candidate quality
- New hire retention
- New hire performance
- Interview quality
- Diversity metrics

**Analyze**:
- What's working?
- Where are delays?
- Are we finding good fits?
- How's retention?
- Are we building diversity?

## Common Hiring Mistakes

**Avoid**:
- Hiring for resume not fit
- Ignoring values misalignment
- Rushing the process
- Ignoring red flags
- Poor onboarding prep
- Not assessing culture fit
- Overstating compensation
- Unclear role expectations
