## Feedback and Coaching Philosophy

Feedback and coaching are essential for development. Regular feedback helps employees understand impact, adjust behavior, and grow. Coaching enables employees to reach their potential.

**Core Principle**: Feedback is gift; coaching is partnership. Both require trust, clarity, and genuine desire to help employee succeed.

## Feedback Framework

### Feedback Principles

1. **Specific**: Tied to observable behavior, not interpretation
2. **Timely**: Given close to event; not delayed
3. **Actionable**: Provides clear path for improvement
4. **Balanced**: Recognition and development
5. **Respectful**: Delivered with respect
6. **Honest**: True feedback, not sugar-coated
7. **Supportive**: Offered to help succeed
8. **Regular**: Ongoing, not once a year

### Feedback Types

**Positive Feedback**:
- Reinforces desired behavior
- Builds confidence
- Motivates
- Should be frequent
- Specific and sincere

**Developmental Feedback**:
- Addresses areas to improve
- Helps growth
- Must be honest
- Delivered respectfully
- Paired with support

**Course-Correction Feedback**:
- Addresses behavior that needs to change
- Clear expectations
- Support for change
- Monitoring
- Consequences if not addressed

## Feedback Process

### Giving Effective Feedback

**Preparation**:
1. Clarify specific behavior
2. Understand impact
3. Know intended outcome
4. Prepare supportive approach
5. Choose right time and place

**Delivery**:

1. **Open**: Set respectful tone
   - "I'd like to share some feedback"
   - "Can we discuss how yesterday's meeting went?"

2. **Describe Behavior**: Be specific
   - "When you interrupted Sarah in the meeting..."
   - "I noticed you missed the deadline on..."

3. **Explain Impact**: What was the result?
   - "It left Sarah feeling dismissed"
   - "It created delay for the team"

4. **Explore**: Understand their perspective
   - "What was going on for you?"
   - "Help me understand..."

5. **Offer Support**: How can you help?
   - "Here's what I see as a path forward..."
   - "What support would help?"

6. **Agree**: Agreement on next steps
   - "Can we agree to try this?"
   - "Let's check in next week"

7. **Close**: Positive close
   - "I appreciate you hearing this"
   - "I believe you can do this"

### Receiving Feedback

**How to receive feedback well**:

1. **Listen**: Really listen to understand
2. **Seek Understanding**: Ask clarifying questions
3. **Accept**: Thank person for feedback
4. **Reflect**: Think about it; don't defend
5. **Act**: Do something with feedback
6. **Follow Up**: Report on progress

**Avoid**:
- Defending yourself
- Making excuses
- Getting defensive
- Dismissing feedback
- Ignoring it

## Coaching Framework

### Coaching Purpose

Coaching is:
- Helping people improve performance
- Unlocking potential
- Developing capability
- Building confidence
- Creating change

Coaching is NOT:
- Telling what to do
- Fixing problems for them
- Therapy
- Mentorship (though related)

### Coaching Relationship

**Effective coaching requires**:
- Trust between coach and coachee
- Willingness to be coached
- Honest communication
- Commitment to improvement
- Regular contact
- Clear goals

### Coaching Process

**Coaching Conversation Steps**:

1. **Establish Goal**
   - What do you want to accomplish?
   - What will success look like?
   - Why is this important?

2. **Assess Current State**
   - Where are you now?
   - What's working?
   - What's not working?
   - What's the gap?

3. **Explore Options**
   - What could you do?
   - What else?
   - What have you tried?
   - What would work best?

4. **Commit to Action**
   - What will you do?
   - By when?
   - What support do you need?
   - How will you measure progress?

5. **Follow Up**
   - How did it go?
   - What did you learn?
   - What's next?
   - How can I support you?

### Coaching Styles

**Different approaches**:

**Directive Coaching**: "Here's what to do"
- When: Employee needs clear direction
- Use: With new employees or urgent issues

**Collaborative Coaching**: "Let's figure this out together"
- When: Employee has capability but needs input
- Use: Most situations

**Empowering Coaching**: "What do you think you should do?"
- When: Employee has capability and confidence
- Use: Developing independence

**Adaptive Coaching**: Adjust approach based on situation and employee

## Manager as Coach

### Manager Coaching Role

Managers coach their teams by:
- Regular one-on-ones
- Asking good questions
- Listening more than telling
- Providing perspective
- Supporting decisions
- Celebrating successes
- Learning from mistakes

### Coaching Conversations

**Regular one-on-one structure**:

1. **Check In** (5 min): How are you?
2. **Progress** (15 min): What's working? What's not?
3. **Support** (20 min): What support do you need?
4. **Development** (10 min): What are you working on developing?
5. **Close** (5 min): Summary and next steps

**Frequency**: Weekly for new employees; bi-weekly for others

## Peer Feedback

### Peer Feedback Process

**Colleagues can give feedback to each other**:
- Same principles apply
- Respectful and specific
- Offered to help
- Well-intentioned
- Timely

**Process**:
- "Can I share something?"
- Describe behavior and impact
- Listen to response
- Support improvement

## 360-Degree Feedback

### Purpose

Gather feedback from multiple sources:
- Manager
- Peers
- Direct reports (if applicable)
- Customers or stakeholders
- Self

### Process

1. **Identify Feedback Providers**: Select diverse raters
2. **Create Survey**: Behavioral questions
3. **Gather Feedback**: Anonymous responses
4. **Analysis**: Patterns and themes
5. **Debrief**: Discussion of results
6. **Development Planning**: Based on feedback

### Use

- Development planning
- Leadership assessment
- Career development
- Succession planning
- Performance evaluation

## Coaching Development

### Training Managers to Coach

**Manager coaching skills**:
- Listening skills
- Questioning skills
- Feedback delivery
- Goal setting
- Support provision
- Accountability

**Organization provides**:
- Coaching training
- Resources and tools
- Modeling by leadership
- Support and feedback
- Coaching on coaching

## Measuring Coaching Effectiveness

**Track**:
- Employee development progress
- Performance improvement
- Engagement scores
- Retention
- Manager coaching frequency
- Employee satisfaction with feedback

**Indicators of Effective Coaching**:
- Employees growing
- Performance improving
- Engagement strong
- Retention good
- Development goals met
- Confidence building

## Feedback and Coaching Culture

**Strong feedback culture**:
- Regular feedback given and received
- Feedback welcome
- Coaching approach
- Continuous development
- Learning from mistakes
- Celebrating growth

**Builds through**:
- Leadership modeling
- Manager training
- Psychological safety
- Recognition of coaching
- Development focus
- Regular practice
