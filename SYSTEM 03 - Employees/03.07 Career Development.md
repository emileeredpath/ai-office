## Career Development Philosophy

Career development is partnership between employee and organization. Organization provides opportunities and support; employee takes initiative and ownership. Clear career paths create motivation and retention.

**Core Principle**: Employees should be able to see a future in organization. Career progression should be based on capability, performance, and readiness, not just tenure.

## Career Paths

### Individual Contributor Track

**Path**: Specialist, Senior Specialist, Principal Specialist

**Focus**:
- Deep expertise in domain
- High-quality delivery
- Technical leadership
- Problem-solving
- Mentoring (at senior levels)

**Progression**:
- Specialist (Early career; developing expertise)
- Senior Specialist (Proven expertise; complex problems)
- Principal Specialist (Deep expertise; organizational influence)

**Advancement Criteria**:
- Technical capability
- Problem-solving skill
- Quality of work
- Initiative and improvement
- Mentoring others
- Domain knowledge

**Compensation**: Increases with seniority and expertise

### Team Lead Track

**Path**: Team Lead, Manager, Senior Manager

**Focus**:
- Team leadership
- People development
- Performance management
- Team coordination
- Execution

**Progression**:
- Team Lead (First-time leader; developing leadership capability)
- Manager (Proven leadership; multiple teams or larger team)
- Senior Manager (Strategic thinking; organizational leadership)

**Advancement Criteria**:
- Leadership capability
- Team performance
- People development
- Business acumen
- Strategic thinking
- Organizational contribution

**Compensation**: Increases with level and impact

### Hybrid Track

**Path**: Specialist + Team Lead roles

**Focus**:
- Expertise with leadership
- Technical strategy
- Team development
- Strategic impact
- Mentoring

**Progression**:
- Senior Specialist with Team Lead responsibility
- Principal with Management responsibility
- Director with Deep Technical Expertise

**Advancement Criteria**:
- Both technical capability and leadership capability
- Dual impact (expertise and people)
- Organizational influence
- Strategic contribution

**Compensation**: Based on both technical level and leadership level

## Career Stages

### Early Career (0-2 years)

**Focus**: Build capability and prove reliability

**Characteristics**:
- Learning role and organization
- Building technical capability
- Proving reliability
- Building relationships
- Understanding culture

**Development Needs**:
- Role-specific training
- Mentorship
- Clear expectations
- Growth opportunities
- Feedback and coaching

**Success Indicators**:
- Capability development
- Reliability and performance
- Cultural fit
- Engagement
- Contribution quality

**Advancement Preparation**:
- Expanded responsibilities
- Challenging projects
- Leadership observation
- Feedback and coaching

### Mid Career (3-7 years)

**Focus**: Deepen expertise and expand impact

**Characteristics**:
- Proven capability in role
- Expanding responsibilities
- Taking on complexity
- Beginning to lead/mentor
- Clear strengths

**Development Needs**:
- Leadership development
- Strategic thinking
- Expanded scope
- Executive exposure
- Advanced training

**Success Indicators**:
- Performance excellence
- Capability expansion
- Initiative and improvement
- Team contribution
- Organizational influence

**Advancement Preparation**:
- Leadership roles
- Strategic projects
- Cross-functional work
- Mentoring others
- Executive visibility

### Senior Career (8+ years)

**Focus**: Strategic impact and organization shaping

**Characteristics**:
- Deep expertise or proven leadership
- Organizational influence
- Strategic thinking
- People development
- Defining direction

**Development Needs**:
- Executive development
- Strategic planning
- Board readiness
- Advanced business skills
- Industry leadership

**Success Indicators**:
- Strategic impact
- Organization development
- Industry influence
- Organizational culture
- Retention and satisfaction

**Advancement Preparation**:
- Executive roles
- Board preparation
- External visibility
- Strategic leadership
- Industry engagement

## Career Conversations

### Annual Career Conversation

**Purpose**: Discuss career progression and aspirations

**Process**:

1. **Reflection**: Where are you in career?
2. **Interests**: What interests you for next role?
3. **Goals**: What are your career goals?
4. **Readiness**: What are you ready for now?
5. **Development**: What development is needed?
6. **Opportunities**: What opportunities exist?
7. **Timeline**: What's realistic timeline?
8. **Next Steps**: What will we do next?

**Participants**: Employee and manager

**Timing**: Often during annual review

**Documentation**: Career development plan

### Career Development Plan

**Includes**:
1. **Current Role**: Where are you now?
2. **Desired Role**: Where do you want to go?
3. **Timeline**: When are you ready?
4. **Development**: What needs to happen?
5. **Opportunities**: What roles/projects will help?
6. **Support**: What support do you need?
7. **Success Criteria**: How will we know readiness?
8. **Actions**: What happens next?

### Informal Career Conversations

**Throughout Year**:
- Career aspirations discussed
- Opportunities highlighted
- Development progress reviewed
- Readiness assessed
- Barriers addressed

**Frequency**: At least semi-annual

## Career Progression Criteria

### Advancement to Senior Individual Contributor

**Requires**:
- Proven excellence in current role
- Deep technical expertise
- High-quality problem-solving
- Initiative and continuous improvement
- Mentoring of junior contributors
- Strategic thinking
- Industry/domain knowledge

**Timeline**: 3-5 years typically

### Advancement to Team Lead

**Requires**:
- Proven performance in individual role
- Leadership capability
- Willingness to develop people
- Communication and interpersonal skills
- Organizational understanding
- Values alignment
- Readiness assessment

**Timeline**: Varies; typically mid-career

### Advancement to Manager

**Requires**:
- Proven leadership as team lead
- People development capability
- Business acumen
- Strategic thinking
- Organizational effectiveness
- Ready for expanded scope

**Timeline**: Typically senior career

### Advancement to Senior Leadership

**Requires**:
- Proven management success
- Strategic capability
- Organization development
- Stakeholder management
- Executive presence
- Ready for executive role

**Timeline**: Varies; demonstrated readiness

## Internal Promotion

### Philosophy

We promote from within when possible because:
- We know capability
- Promotes retention
- Builds organizational culture
- Develops leadership pipeline
- Cost-effective

### Process

1. **Identify opportunity**: Open role
2. **Announce internally**: First opportunity to internal candidates
3. **Evaluation**: Assess candidates fairly
4. **Selection**: Choose best fit
5. **Transition**: Support role transition
6. **Development**: Provide support in new role

### Promotion Timing

**Best practices**:
- Promote when ready, not too early
- Ensure capability for new role
- Avoid promotion for wrong reasons (pressure, politics)
- Support success in new role
- Don't create skill gaps in old role

## Lateral Moves

### Purpose

Lateral moves:
- Build organizational knowledge
- Develop diverse capability
- Create career options
- Address individual interests
- Meet organizational needs

### When Appropriate

- When employee wants new experience
- When organization needs capability
- When skills transfer well
- When role creates good development
- When timing works for business

### Process

- Discussion of opportunity
- Assessment of readiness
- Planning for transition
- Support in new role
- No stigma or penalty

## Career Development Resources

**Organization provides**:
- Career counseling
- Development planning
- Training and courses
- Mentorship
- Stretch assignments
- Cross-functional projects
- Leadership programs
- Industry engagement
- Educational support

**Employee provides**:
- Initiative and ownership
- Follow-through on development
- Readiness to learn
- Engagement with opportunities
- Honest self-assessment

## Retention Through Career Development

**Career development supports retention by**:
- Creating clear opportunities
- Enabling growth
- Providing challenge
- Building engagement
- Creating investment in employee
- Developing sense of future

**Organizations with clear career paths have better retention.**

## Succession Planning

### Organizational Focus

**For key roles**:
- Identify critical positions
- Identify successors
- Develop successors
- Maintain bench strength
- Prepare organization

### Individual Focus

**Each employee is**:
- Developed for expanded roles
- Prepared for advancement
- Supported in growth
- Included in succession planning (if applicable)
