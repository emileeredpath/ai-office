## Employee Identity

An employee is a person who contributes their expertise, energy, and commitment to the organization's mission and vision.

**Core Understanding**: Employees are not resources to be managed but people with capabilities, aspirations, and values who choose to contribute to organizational success. The relationship is reciprocal: employees contribute work and commitment; the organization provides opportunity, support, and recognition.

## Employee Classification

### Full-Time Employees

**Definition**: Committed to organization on ongoing basis

**Characteristics**:
- Primary employment relationship
- Dedicated focus on organizational work
- Full benefits and support
- Career development opportunity
- Long-term relationship

**Commitment**: Typically 40+ hours per week

**Relationship**: Core organizational members

### Part-Time Employees

**Definition**: Committed to organization on reduced-hours basis

**Characteristics**:
- Flexible schedule
- Focused contribution area
- Limited benefits (varies by region and regulation)
- Development opportunities
- Medium-term relationship

**Commitment**: Typically 20-40 hours per week

**Relationship**: Extended organizational members

### Contractors

**Definition**: Engaged for specific work or project

**Characteristics**:
- Defined scope of work
- Time-limited engagement
- Specific deliverables
- No employment benefits
- Specific expertise

**Commitment**: Varies by engagement

**Relationship**: External contributors with defined role

## Employee Ecosystem

### Hierarchy

**Level 1: Contributors**
- Individual contributors
- Specialists
- Operational employees
- Execute defined work
- Report to team leads or managers

**Level 2: Team Leads**
- Manage small teams (3-8 people)
- Responsible for team performance
- Develop team members
- Report to managers or leadership

**Level 3: Managers**
- Manage multiple teams or functions
- Responsible for area performance
- Develop team leaders
- Report to directors or senior leadership

**Level 4: Leadership**
- Set organizational direction
- Responsible for major functions
- Develop managers
- Report to executive leadership

**Level 5: Executive Leadership**
- Set overall organizational strategy
- Responsible for organizational success
- Develop leadership team
- Accountable to board or stakeholders

### Specialist Categories

Employees can be specialists in various functional areas:

**Operations**:

**Delivery/Project Management**:

**People/Culture**:

- People management

**Strategy/Planning**:
- Strategic planning

**Finance/Administration**:
- Financial management

## Employee Relationship Lifecycle

### Stage 1: Recruitment

**Focus**: Identify and attract talent

**Organization Role**:
- Define role and requirements
- Communicate opportunity
- Evaluate candidates
- Extend offer

**Candidate Role**:
- Explore opportunity
- Evaluate fit
- Assess alignment
- Make decision

**Outcome**: Mutual agreement to employment

### Stage 2: Onboarding

**Focus**: Integrate new employee into organization

**Organization Role**:
- Provide orientation
- Establish relationships
- Clarify expectations
- Build capability

**Employee Role**:
- Learn organization
- Build relationships
- Understand role
- Demonstrate capability

**Duration**: Typically 3 months (with formal program)

**Outcome**: Employee ready to contribute independently

### Stage 3: Contribution

**Focus**: Employee makes meaningful contribution

**Organization Role**:
- Provide direction and support
- Recognize contribution
- Provide feedback
- Enable success

**Employee Role**:
- Deliver quality work
- Take ownership
- Continuous learning
- Contribute to team

**Duration**: Throughout employment

**Outcome**: Ongoing value creation

### Stage 4: Development

**Focus**: Employee grows and develops

**Organization Role**:
- Identify development needs
- Provide learning opportunities
- Mentor and coach
- Create advancement opportunities

**Employee Role**:
- Seek development
- Take on challenges
- Apply learnings
- Contribute to others' development

**Duration**: Throughout employment

**Outcome**: Increasing capability and contribution

### Stage 5: Transition

**Focus**: Employee transitions out of role or organization

**Organization Role**:
- Support transition
- Knowledge transfer
- Positive closure
- Reference and recognition

**Employee Role**:
- Support transition
- Knowledge transfer
- Responsible exit
- Maintain relationships

**Duration**: Varies (weeks to months)

**Outcome**: Professional closure

## Employee Success

Employee success is defined by:

1. **Capability**: Employee has skills and knowledge to do job well
2. **Performance**: Employee delivers quality work consistently
3. **Engagement**: Employee is energized and committed
4. **Development**: Employee is growing and developing
5. **Contribution**: Employee adds value to organization
6. **Alignment**: Employee's work and values align with organization
7. **Recognition**: Employee feels valued and appreciated
8. **Growth**: Employee has path forward in organization

## Employee Responsibilities to Organization

1. **Deliver Quality Work**: Complete assigned responsibilities well
2. **Maintain Professionalism**: Conduct self professionally
3. **Communicate Transparently**: Share relevant information
4. **Support Team**: Help colleagues succeed
5. **Embrace Culture**: Live organizational values
6. **Continuous Learning**: Develop yourself
7. **Accountability**: Own your results
8. **Integrity**: Act with integrity always

## Organization's Responsibility to Employees

1. **Clear Direction**: Define role and expectations
2. **Support and Resources**: Provide what's needed to succeed
3. **Fair Treatment**: Equitable decisions and opportunities
4. **Development**: Enable growth and development
5. **Recognition**: Acknowledge contributions
6. **Safety**: Psychological and physical safety
7. **Communication**: Transparent, regular communication
8. **Career Path**: Opportunity for advancement
