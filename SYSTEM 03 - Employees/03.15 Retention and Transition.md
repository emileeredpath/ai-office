## Retention Philosophy

Retention begins at hiring and continues throughout employment. Strong retention indicates organizational health. Losing good people is costly and disruptive. Retention is strategic priority.

**Core Principle**: Invest in employees from day one; build commitment and belonging; create reasons to stay. Retention is built through excellent management, meaningful work, and opportunity.

## Why Retention Matters

### Cost of Turnover

**Replacing employee costs**:
- Recruitment costs
- Training and onboarding
- Productivity ramp-up time (30-90 days)
- Institutional knowledge loss
- Team disruption
- Customer impact (if applicable)
- Total cost: 50-200% of annual salary

**Impact**:
- Significant financial impact
- Operational disruption
- Culture impact
- Knowledge loss
- Team morale

### Benefits of Retention

**Retaining good employees**:
- Continuity and stability
- Institutional knowledge
- Relationship maintenance
- Cost savings
- Culture preservation
- Capability building
- Customer relationships

## Retention Monitoring

### Retention Metrics

**Track**:
- Retention rate (overall)
- Retention by department
- Retention by level
- Turnover rate
- Tenure
- Regrettable vs. non-regrettable turnover

**Analyze**:
- Who are we losing?
- When are they leaving?
- Why are they leaving?
- What's our trend?
- How do we compare?

### Exit Interviews

**When employee leaves**:
- Conduct exit interview
- Understand reasons for leaving
- Gather feedback
- Identify patterns
- Identify improvements
- Leave positively

**Exit Interview Questions**:
- Why are you leaving?
- What was your experience?
- What could we improve?
- Would you consider returning?
- Will you refer others?
- What will you miss?
- Lessons for us?

### Engagement Surveys

**Annual engagement survey**:
- Measure engagement
- Identify issues
- Assess retention risk
- Identify key drivers
- Plan improvements

## Transition Management

### Resignation Process

**When employee resigns**:

1. **Acceptance**: Accept resignation professionally
2. **Discussion**: Understand decision
3. **Final Date**: Confirm last day (typically 2+ weeks)
4. **Transition Plan**: Plan for handoff
5. **Communication**: Tell relevant people
6. **Support**: Provide support during transition

**Manager Role**:
- Professional acknowledgment
- Gratitude for contribution
- Understanding of situation
- Transition planning
- No bad feelings
- Support through transition

### Knowledge Transfer

**During final weeks**:

1. **Documentation**: Document key information
2. **Training**: Train replacement or team
3. **Relationship Transfer**: Introduce to key contacts
4. **Project Handoff**: Hand off projects
5. **System Access**: Ensure replacement has access
6. **Final Review**: Final check-in

**What to capture**:
- Key processes
- Important contacts
- System information
- Project status
- Decisions and rationale
- Unfinished work
- Lessons learned

### Final Paycheck and Exit

**Legal and administrative**:
- Final paycheck including accrued time off
- Benefits continuation information (COBRA, etc.)
- Reference confirmation
- Tax forms
- Positive exit
- Return of equipment

**Exit Conversation**:
- Gratitude
- Positive acknowledgment
- Well-wishes
- Reference availability
- Door left open

### Alumni Relationships

**After departure**:
- Maintain professional relationship
- Reference provided
- Alumni network invited
- Positive relationship
- Possible return to company

## Reducing Regrettable Turnover

### Identification

**Who are we losing that we want to keep?**
- High performers
- Key role holders
- Long-tenured employees
- People with institutional knowledge
- High-potential employees

### Retention Efforts

**For at-risk employees**:
- Career conversation
- Development opportunity
- Compensation review
- Role expansion
- Leadership exposure
- Mentorship
- Special project
- Why they matter communication

### Intervention Early

**Signs someone may leave**:
- Reduced engagement
- Lack of development conversation
- Looking around
- Performance dip
- Disengagement from team
- Absence of career discussion

**Response**:
- Manager conversation
- Understand what's needed
- Address concerns
- Develop plan
- Stay connected
- Regular check-ins

## Managing Separation

### Performance-Based Separation

**When performance inadequate**:
1. Clear expectations
2. Performance improvement plan
3. Support and monitoring
4. Assessment
5. Decision
6. Professional separation
7. Transition

### Layoffs or Reductions

**If reduction needed**:
- Criteria for selection (never personal)
- Notification process (respectful)
- Severance (generous if possible)
- Outplacement support
- Transition support
- Communication
- Remaining team support

### Termination for Cause

**For serious violations**:
- Clear policy
- Investigation
- Fair process
- Legal compliance
- Professional termination
- Final pay and benefits
- Reference management

## Building Retention Culture

**Organization builds retention culture**:
- Leadership commitment
- Manager training
- Clear career paths
- Development focus
- Recognition and appreciation
- Fair compensation
- Psychological safety
- Meaningful work
- Belonging culture

**Retention is intentional.**
