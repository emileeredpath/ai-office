# SYSTEM 03 - Employees

**Individual Contributor and Team Management**

SYSTEM 03 - Employees defines how individual contributors succeed, teams operate effectively, and the organization builds a strong workforce. It provides the human foundation that enables organizational excellence.

---

## System Overview

SYSTEM 03 - Employees is the employee-focused system that defines:

1. **Employee Identity**: Who employees are and their role in organization
2. **Employee Engagement**: Meaningful work, development, and contribution
3. **Hiring and Onboarding**: Building and integrating talent
4. **Performance and Development**: Enabling success and growth
5. **Career Paths**: Growth and advancement opportunity
6. **Team Dynamics**: How teams operate effectively
7. **Conflict Resolution**: Addressing challenges constructively
8. **Recognition and Feedback**: Acknowledging contribution and enabling growth
9. **Compensation and Benefits**: Fair reward and employee support
10. **Retention**: Keeping talented people

## Document Structure

### Foundational Documents

#### [03.00 Who is an Employee](03.00%20Who%20is%20an%20Employee.md)
Defines employee identity, classification (full-time, part-time, contractor), role in organization, and the reciprocal relationship. Establishes employee value proposition and lifecycle.

#### [03.01 Employee Purpose](03.01%20Employee%20Purpose.md)
Defines how employees contribute to organizational mission and their individual purpose. Establishes five core employee purposes and purpose development across career stages.

#### [03.02 Employee Responsibilities](03.02%20Employee%20Responsibilities.md)
Defines eight core responsibilities all employees carry: quality work, ownership, transparency, respect, values alignment, continuous learning, team support, and ethical conduct. Responsibilities by level.

#### [03.03 Employee Authority](03.03%20Employee%20Authority.md)
Defines decision authority at different levels: direct authority (individual), consultation authority (team lead), approval authority (manager), and executive authority. Escalation process.

### Talent Acquisition and Integration

#### [03.04 Hiring and Recruitment](03.04%20Hiring%20and%20Recruitment.md)
Defines systematic hiring process: planning, recruitment, interviewing, selection, and offer. Hiring criteria emphasizing values alignment and capability.

#### [03.05 Onboarding](03.05%20Onboarding.md)
Defines systematic onboarding from pre-arrival through 90-day review. Creates strong foundation for new employee success and cultural integration.

### Development and Performance

#### [03.06 Performance and Development](03.06%20Performance%20and%20Development.md)
Defines performance management and individual development. Covers performance dimensions, reviews (annual, mid-year, ongoing), development planning, and underperformance management.

#### [03.07 Career Development](03.07%20Career%20Development.md)
Defines career paths and development opportunities. Covers individual contributor, team lead, manager, and executive tracks. Career conversations and progression criteria.

### Team and Collaboration

#### [03.08 Team Dynamics](03.08%20Team%20Dynamics.md)
Defines how high-performing teams operate. Covers team characteristics, formation stages, communication, cross-functional teams, and team leadership.

#### [03.09 Conflict Resolution](03.09%20Conflict%20Resolution.md)
Defines processes for resolving conflicts constructively. Covers conflict types, resolution levels (direct, manager mediation, HR involvement), and prevention strategies.

### Engagement and Recognition

#### [03.10 Engagement](03.10%20Engagement.md)
Defines employee engagement and drivers: meaningful work, clear direction, supportive manager, development, recognition, psychological safety, belonging, and trust. Measurement and building engagement culture.

#### [03.11 Feedback and Coaching](03.11%20Feedback%20and%20Coaching.md)
Defines feedback and coaching processes. Covers feedback principles, giving and receiving feedback, coaching relationships, and coaching conversations.

#### [03.12 Recognition](03.12%20Recognition.md)
Defines recognition systems and practices. Covers recognition types (peer, manager, leadership, public, private), recognition programs, and creating recognition culture.

### Compensation and Support

#### [03.13 Compensation](03.13%20Compensation.md)
Defines compensation strategy and practices. Covers salary, bonuses, equity, compensation reviews, and performance-based pay. Compensation by level.

#### [03.14 Benefits and Wellbeing](03.14%20Benefits%20and%20Wellbeing.md)
Defines employee benefits and wellbeing programs. Covers health insurance, retirement, disability, paid time off, professional development, and wellbeing initiatives.

#### [03.15 Retention and Transition](03.15%20Retention%20and%20Transition.md)
Defines retention strategies and transition management. Covers retention drivers, monitoring, knowledge transfer, and alumni relationships.

## Key Concepts

### Employee as System

Employees are part of interconnected systems:
- Individual (purpose, development, performance)
- Team (dynamics, collaboration, culture)
- Organizational (strategy, culture, mission)
- External (customers, partners, market)

### Reciprocal Relationship

Employment is mutual benefit:
- Organization provides: work, development, recognition, support
- Employee provides: capability, commitment, values alignment, contribution

### Development-Focused

Organization invests in employee development:
- Continuous learning
- Career progression
- Capability growth
- Leadership development

### Engagement-Driven

Organizational success depends on engagement:
- Meaningful work
- Development
- Recognition
- Supportive culture
- Clear purpose
