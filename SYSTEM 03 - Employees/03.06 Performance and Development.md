## Performance and Development Philosophy

Individual performance and development are interconnected. Performance management measures what people deliver; development enables them to deliver more. Both are essential for organizational and individual success.

**Core Principle**: Performance and development conversations should be supportive, honest, and forward-focused. They're about enabling success, not just evaluating past performance.

## Performance Framework

### Performance Dimensions

**Each employee is assessed on**:

1. **Quality**: Work done well and to standard
2. **Delivery**: Work completed on time
3. **Engagement**: Effort and commitment
4. **Collaboration**: Teamwork and support
5. **Growth**: Continuous learning and development
6. **Values Alignment**: Living organizational values
7. **Initiative**: Taking ownership and improving

### Performance Standards

**Expectations**:
- Consistently meet quality standards
- Deliver work on schedule
- Provide effort and commitment
- Collaborate effectively
- Seek continuous learning
- Model values
- Take initiative

**Performance Levels**:
- **Exceeds Expectations**: Consistently exceeds standards; high impact
- **Meets Expectations**: Consistently meets standards; reliable contributor
- **Needs Improvement**: Meets some standards; gaps in performance
- **Below Expectations**: Consistently below standards; performance issues

### Performance Measurement

**How We Measure**:
- Clear success criteria (set at start of period)
- Regular monitoring throughout period
- Observation and feedback
- Results achieved
- Peer and team feedback
- Self-assessment

## Performance Reviews

### Annual Review

**Timing**: January typically; aligned with annual planning

**Process**:

1. **Preparation** (1 week before)
   - Employee: Self-assessment against goals
   - Manager: Review performance data
   - Both: Gather feedback from colleagues

2. **Review Conversation** (1-2 hours)
   - Discuss year in review
   - Assess performance against goals
   - Discuss strengths and development areas
   - Discuss values alignment
   - Discuss initiative and contributions
   - Assess overall performance

3. **Outcomes**
   - Performance rating
   - Compensation discussion (if applicable)
   - Development plan
   - Next year goals

**Participants**: Employee and direct manager

**Documentation**: Written performance review

### Mid-Year Review

**Timing**: July typically

**Purpose**: Check progress on annual goals and development

**Process**:
- Discuss first half year
- Assess progress toward goals
- Adjust goals if needed
- Discuss development progress
- Address any concerns
- Confirm direction for second half

**Participants**: Employee and direct manager

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes

### Ongoing Performance Feedback

**Throughout Year**:
- Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins
- Regular feedback on work
- Recognition of strong work
- Coaching on areas to improve
- Support and resources provided
- Course correction as needed

**Frequency**: At least weekly

**Owner**: Direct manager

## Development Framework

### Development Areas

**Employees develop in**:
1. **Technical Skills**: Depth in role-specific capabilities
2. **Functional Skills**: Capabilities applicable across roles
3. **Leadership Skills**: Ability to lead and influence
4. **Business Skills**: Business acumen and understanding
5. **Soft Skills**: Communication, collaboration, emotional intelligence
6. **Domain Knowledge**: Understanding of industry and domain

### Development Approach

**Methods**:
- On-the-job learning through challenging projects
- Mentorship and coaching
- Training and courses
- Reading and self-study
- Peer learning
- Formal education (if applicable)
- Rotation through roles

### Development Planning

**Annual Process**:

1. **Assessment**: What are development needs?
2. **Goals**: What do you want to develop?
3. **Plan**: How will you develop it?
4. **Resources**: What support do you need?
5. **Metrics**: How will we know progress?
6. **Timeline**: When will this happen?

**Development Plan includes**:
- Development goals (2-3 typically)
- Why this development matters
- How you'll develop
- Resources and support needed
- Success criteria
- Timeline

**Ownership**: Employee owns development; manager supports

### Development Opportunities

**Organization provides**:
- Challenging projects
- Stretch assignments
- Mentorship
- Coaching
- Training budget
- Learning time
- Exposure to leadership
- Cross-functional projects
- Rotation opportunities

**Employee provides**:
- Initiative to learn
- Effort to develop
- Openness to feedback
- Follow-through on development
- Application of learnings

## Individual Goal Setting

### Goal Framework

**Each employee sets**:
- 3-5 annual goals aligned with organizational objectives
- 2-3 development goals
- Quarterly objectives

**Goal Characteristics**:
- Clear and specific
- Measurable
- Achievable but challenging
- Aligned with organizational strategy
- Aligned with values

### Goal Types

**Performance Goals**: Work deliverables and objectives

**Development Goals**: Learning and capability growth

**Improvement Goals**: Areas to improve performance

**Values Goals**: Embodying values in specific ways

**Initiative Goals**: Improvements or innovations to pursue

### Goal Review

**Quarterly**: Review progress and adjust as needed

**Annual**: Full assessment against goals

**Ongoing**: Weekly check-ins on progress

## Managing Underperformance

### Recognition

**When Performance Falls Short**:

1. **Clarity**: Ensure employee understands expectation
2. **Discussion**: Discuss performance gap
3. **Understanding**: Understand root cause
4. **Support**: What support is needed?
5. **Plan**: Specific improvement plan
6. **Monitoring**: Regular check-ins
7. **Assessment**: Is performance improving?

### Performance Improvement Plan

**When Needed**: Documented underperformance

**Process**:
1. Written plan with clear expectations
2. Specific behaviors to address
3. Timeline for improvement (typically 30-90 days)
4. Support and resources provided
5. Regular check-ins (weekly or bi-weekly)
6. Clear success criteria
7. Final assessment

**Outcomes**:
- Performance improves → Continue normal employment
- Partial improvement → Extended plan or role change
- No improvement → Further action (discipline, termination)

## Recognizing Strong Performance

### Recognition

**Strong performers are**:
- Recognized formally
- Recognized publicly
- Recognized by leadership
- Recognized financially (if possible)
- Given expanded opportunities

### Advancement

**High performers advance through**:
- Expanded responsibilities
- Leadership opportunities
- Special projects
- Development into leadership role
- Promotion when appropriate

## Development and Performance Connection

### Performance Enables Development

- Strong performance creates capacity for development
- Success with current role allows stretch assignments
- Reliability enables risk-taking

### Development Enables Performance

- New skills improve performance
- Learning improves capability
- Development increases confidence

### Virtuous Cycle

**High performers → Develop → Stronger performers → More development → Leadership pipeline**

## Performance Conversations

### Key Principles

1. **Honesty**: Feedback is honest and fair
2. **Respect**: Feedback is delivered with respect
3. **Specificity**: Feedback is specific and observable
4. **Forward-Looking**: Focused on improvement, not blame
5. **Supportive**: Offered in spirit of support
6. **Two-Way**: Dialogue, not monologue
7. **Action-Oriented**: Leads to clear actions

### Performance Conversation Structure

1. **Open**: Set tone; explain purpose
2. **Self-Assessment**: Employee perspective first
3. **Manager Input**: Manager feedback and observations
4. **Discussion**: Dialogue on performance
5. **Strengths**: Acknowledge what's working
6. **Development**: Discuss areas to improve
7. **Support**: Discuss what support is needed
8. **Plan**: Agree on next steps
9. **Close**: Summary and closing

## Performance Metrics

**Track**:
- Performance ratings distribution
- Development plan completion
- Goal achievement rates
- Performance improvement plan success
- Internal promotion rates
- Employee satisfaction with performance process

**Analyze**:
- Are expectations clear?
- Are we supporting success?
- Is development happening?
- Are managers giving feedback?
- Are conversations effective?
